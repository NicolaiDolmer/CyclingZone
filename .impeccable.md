## Design Context

### Users
Cycling enthusiasts and fantasy sports gamers, Danish-speaking, who understand professional cycling. Playing a season-long team management simulation: auctions, rider management, squad building, finances. Engaged daily users during active seasons. The audience is adults who take the game seriously — competitive, strategic.

### Brand Personality
Tactical, grounded, spirited. Not goofy, not gambling-site aggressive. Happy and professional — like following a sport you love. Think: a well-run sports desk, not an arcade.

### Aesthetic Direction
Reference: Football Manager — dark structured navigation panel + lighter content area. Professional sports management aesthetic: data-dense but legible, never decorative for decoration's sake.

**Theme**: Dark navy sidebar, warm cream main content area, white cards. Trophy gold (`#e8c547`) is the single accent — used for active nav states, key numbers, filled badges. Dimmed to `text-amber-700` when appearing as text on white surfaces.

**Anti-references**: Gambling sites (aggressive reds, flashing), goofy cartoon UI, pure sterile spreadsheet.

### Design Principles
1. **Dark sidebar, light content** — navigation is dark navy; main content uses a warm off-white palette with white cards
2. **Labels vs links** — nav section headers must be visually subordinate to nav items: smaller, dimmer, clearly non-interactive in appearance
3. **Data-first, decoration-last** — rider/stats table pages can look like modern Excel; decoration only serves communication
4. **Token-based color** — all colors go through CSS custom properties; no hardcoded hex values in components
5. **Professional, not goofy** — confident, clear, occasionally warm — emoji only for status icons, never for personality
