What each variant locks in

Pick A (None) → most premium, most restrained. Trust the lines + favicon. Brand systems like Whoop and Rapha work this way — they say only what's needed and let scarcity do the talking. Future site UI can carry the lines as a system element across cards, sections, dividers. This is the recommended call.

Pick B (Wheel-ring accent) → adds one cycling cue via substitution (not addition). Subtle but readable as a wheel/hub. Builds a small system: the ring can also be reused as a UI motif (toggle, status indicator, section anchor). Most integrated.

Pick C (Period dot) → adds one cycling cue as a punctuation flourish. Even smaller mark than B. Works if you read it as a hub; can also be read as "you are here". Lowest visual cost but also lowest read-rate.

Pick D (Both) → maximum DNA on top of the locked system. Risk: drifts from restrained-premium toward decorated-logo. Only pick if A feels noticeably too quiet next to B/C.

My read: A. The locked system already over-delivers cycling-signal between the lines and the stacked favicon. Restraint here amplifies premium feel. But you set the bar — if B or C calls to you, that means the lines+favicon alone aren't enough for your taste, which is valid signal.
