Below: best-practice principle, then head-to-head wordmark comparison, then reference table from mature brands.

i
Best-practice principle

"One font everywhere" is the beginner version of brand consistency. Mature brand systems use 2-3 carefully-chosen fonts where each does its specific job. Display fonts (Bebas Neue) optimize for memorability + personality at large sizes. UI fonts (Inter Tight) optimize for legibility + density at small sizes.

The rule is function-matching, not uniformity. Bebas + Inter is a sibling pair: both sans-serif, both modern, both precise. They work together because they share temperament — not because they're identical.

My read

Variant B (Inter Tight Black CZ, plain) — recommended.

Bebas Neue (A, C) is condensed and beautiful at large sizes, but its strokes thin out at 16×16 — same root cause as the failure case. Inter Tight Black has thicker strokes that survive the worst-case test. Two-letter "CZ" doesn't need decoration to feel premium — it just needs to read. The dash (C, D) adds a brand-DNA tie-in but eats pixels at 16px, where every pixel matters.

Small + large favicons being different forms is standard practice — Stripe uses serif wordmark + sans favicon, Notion uses one mark at small and another at large. The brand-coherence anchor isn't "same font everywhere", it's the yellow + navy + rounded-square system, which holds across F1a and F1b regardless of which variant you pick.
