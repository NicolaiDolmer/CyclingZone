Why the jersey is yellow

The leader's jersey is yellow because L'Auto, the paper that founded the Tour in 1903, printed on yellow stock. We kept that yellow as the color of winning.

A's exact canvas color #f5edcf, used as one deliberate story-section. Also the natural OG/social-share image.

My recommendation

Your instinct is right: go C (or the new E), and let A's yellow be the leader. For a data-dense manager game where you just chose Inter Tight for density, a calm near-white canvas keeps the tables readable and lets the gold mean something when it appears. Splitting "calm canvas" from "gold = winning" is stronger than making the whole app yellow: every screen stays legible, and yellow keeps its punch as the maillot-jaune signal.

C vs the new E: C (#faf8ee) is a clean near-white, slightly flat. E "Chalk" (#f4f2ec) is my refinement: a touch more warmth and presence so it reads as a considered premium surface (Whoop-warm) rather than a default near-white, while staying just as data-friendly. It also sits cleaner against the gold than the current cream, which is a bit beige/muddy. E is current-cream done right.

So my pick: E as the canvas + yellow-jersey-as-leader for A's story. If E feels too close to C to bother, C is a perfectly safe second. Either way we lock "gold = leading" as a brand rule.
