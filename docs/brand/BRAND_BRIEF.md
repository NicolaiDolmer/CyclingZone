# Brand Brief — Cycling Zone

> **Purpose:** Single-source DNA for every visual identity decision (logo, favicon, marketing, social, Discord).
> **Owner:** Nicolai Dolmer Mikkelsen (founder)
> **Established:** 2026-05-18
> **Status:** Working draft. Update when DNA shifts, not when assets change.

---

## 1. Brand essence (one sentence)

A founder-led cycling manager MMO where strategy beats spending. Premium is optional. Fairness is the promise.

## 2. Audience

**Primary:** International cycling fans, 25-45, who already love cycling tactics. They watch the Tour de France with notes, they play Pro Cycling Manager, they follow Strava leaderboards with opinions. They want depth, not loot boxes. They will pay for things they believe in, but they smell pay-to-win from a mile away.

**Secondary:** Strategists from other sports manager games (Football Manager, OOTP Baseball, Hattrick) who are curious about cycling as a tactical sport.

**What they respond to:** Honesty. Restraint. Premium without arrogance. Build-in-public transparency over corporate polish.

## 3. Personality — 5 adjectives

1. **Refined** — premium feel, not flashy. Restraint over decoration. Whoop, not GoPro.
2. **Strategic** — manager-game DNA. Signals depth, planning, layered decisions.
3. **Honest** — founder-led. No marketing-speak. What you see is what you get.
4. **Precise** — like a well-tuned bike. Every visual element earns its place.
5. **Approachable** — premium but not exclusive. Free game, optional premium.

## 4. What we are NOT (anti-patterns)

- ❌ **Esports/gaming aggressive** — italic-glow, neon, gamer-shouting. Reads as Twitch clip-bait.
- ❌ **Corporate enterprise** — sterile, generic-tech, "we're a serious SaaS". Reads as B2B insurance.
- ❌ **Cartoon/playful** — Duolingo-fun, Notion-friendly. Too light for premium tier credibility.
- ❌ **Pure cycling lifestyle brand** — Rapha-luxury, Pas Normal-fashion. We are a *game*, not a kit.
- ❌ **Big-team faceless** — Nicolai's stemme is the brand. Not "Cycling Zone Team". Not "we at CZ".

## 7. Logo principles — non-negotiable

1. **Scalable** from 16×16 favicon to 5m billboard. Vector-only (SVG source).
2. **Distinct silhouette** — recognizable as pure black silhouette on white.
3. **Subtle cycling DNA** — present but not literal. No obvious cyclist illustration.
4. **Wordmark-first identity** — monogram is derived, never the primary mark in marketing.
5. **No effects that date** — no gradients, no glow, no italic-as-style, no 3D bevels.
6. **Works in one color** — black on white, white on navy, yellow on navy.
7. **Memorable in 5 seconds** — a viewer can sketch it from memory next day.

## 10. Validation tests — before approving any final mark

Run all 5. If it fails any, iterate.

1. **Size test:** print at 16×16 and 100mm. Still readable both?
2. **Silhouette test:** fill with pure black. Recognizable shape?
3. **Peer test:** put it next to Whoop, Strava, Rapha. Does it hold its own?
4. **Recall test:** show to 3 cycling fans for 5 seconds. Can they sketch it tomorrow?
5. **Loyalty test:** would they wear it on a $50 t-shirt? (If no, your brand has no fan-pull yet.)
